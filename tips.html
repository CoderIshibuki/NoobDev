How to type like a master


1. Find the "Home Row"

This is the most important rule. Your fingers should always rest on the middle row of the keyboard (A, S, D, F and J, K, L, ;).

  • Look closely: The F and J keys usually have small bumps on them.
  • The Trick: Use these bumps to find your place without looking down. Your index fingers go on the bumps.

2. Accuracy is better than speed

Do not try to type fast immediately. If you type fast but make mistakes, you have to stop and use "Backspace." This slows you down.

  • Practice: Type slowly and try to get 100% accuracy. Speed will happen naturally as your muscles remember the keys.

3. Use all your fingers

Many beginners only use two or four fingers (often called "hunt and peck").

  • The Goal: You should use all 10 fingers. Each finger has a specific area of the keyboard it is responsible for. This means your hands travel less distance, which makes you faster.

4. Don't look at the keyboard

5. Sit correctly

Typing is a physical activity. To type for a long time without pain:

  • Sit up straight with your back against the chair.
  • Keep your feet flat on the floor.
  • Keep your elbows bent at a 90-degree angle.
  • Important: Do not rest your wrists on the table while typing. Let them hover slightly so your fingers can reach the keys easily.
