Proper Posture and Monitor Position

Sit straight with your feet flat on the floor. Keep your monitor at eye level to reduce neck strain. Your wrists should hover above the keyboard, not rest on it.

Correct Hand Placement

Place your fingers on the "Home Row" (ASDF for left hand, JKL; for right hand). Feel for the small bumps on the F and J keys to find your position without looking.

Learn Touch Typing

Touch typing means typing without looking at the keys. It relies on muscle memory. Start slow, focus on hitting the right keys with the right fingers.

Avoid Looking at the Keyboard

Resist the urge to peek! Cover your hands with a towel if you have to. Looking at the screen helps you spot errors immediately.

Use All 10 Fingers

Don't just "hunt and peck" with two fingers. Each finger has its own zone on the keyboard. Using all fingers reduces distance traveled and increases speed.

Practice Regularly

Consistency is key. Even 15 minutes a day is better than a 2-hour session once a week. Use tools like NoobDev to track your progress.

Use Keyboard Shortcuts

Master shortcuts like Ctrl+C (Copy), Ctrl+V (Paste), Ctrl+Z (Undo). They save massive amounts of time compared to using the mouse.

Take Breaks

Typing fatigue leads to mistakes. Follow the 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. Stretch your hands too!

Focus on Accuracy First

Speed comes naturally with accuracy. If you type fast but make many errors, you waste time correcting them. Aim for 100% accuracy, then speed up.

Set Realistic Goals

Don't expect to jump from 30 WPM to 100 WPM overnight. Set small milestones (e.g., reach 40 WPM this week) and celebrate them.

Use CTRL + Backspace

Instead of hitting Backspace multiple times to delete a word, hold CTRL and press Backspace to delete the entire word at once.

Master Shift Key

Use the Shift key on the opposite hand of the letter you are typing. For example, hold Left Shift to type 'P' (right hand) and Right Shift to type 'A' (left hand).
