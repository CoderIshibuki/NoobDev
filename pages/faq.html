What is a typing test?

A typing test measures a person's speed and accuracy when typing text.

What is a typing test for?

It helps evaluate your current skill level, track progress over time, and identify areas where you need improvement.

Why practice typing?

Typing is a fundamental digital skill. Faster typing saves time and boosts productivity.

How long do I need to practice?

Consistency beats intensity. 10-15 minutes of focused practice daily is sufficient.

What is WPM?

WPM stands for "Words Per Minute". It is calculated by taking total characters typed divided by 5.

What are net keystrokes?

Net keystrokes count only the correct characters typed.

What are gross keystrokes?

Gross keystrokes count every single key press, including mistakes.

What are total keystrokes?

Total keystrokes include every key press made during the test.

Accuracy vs Actual Accuracy?

Accuracy ignores corrected errors, while Actual Accuracy counts all mistakes made.

What is touch typing?

Touch typing is typing without looking at the keyboard, relying on muscle memory.

How to practice touch typing?

Place fingers on the Home Row (ASDF JKL;) and practice pressing keys without looking.

What is muscle memory?

It's the ability to reproduce a movement automatically through repetition without conscious thought.

How to type faster?

Don't look at keys, focus on accuracy, and maintain good posture.

What is the average typing speed?

The average speed is around 40-50 WPM.
